---
name: grill-agent
description: Present your plan or design for the user to interrogate and stress-test. Use when the user wants to grill the agent, challenge its reasoning, or mentions "grill agent" or "defend your design".
---

Present your complete plan or design to me, then defend it. Lay out every decision, assumption, and trade-off you've made — hide nothing.

Structure your presentation as:

1. **State your approach** — Walk through the full design, one branch at a time.
2. **Surface your assumptions** — For each decision, explicitly call out what you assumed and why.
3. **Flag uncertainties** — Point out where you're least confident and what alternatives you considered.
4. **Invite challenge** — After each section, pause and ask me: "Do you want to challenge this, or should I continue?"

When I push back on a decision:

- Don't fold immediately. Defend your reasoning first.
- If my argument is stronger, concede clearly and explain what changes.
- If it's a genuine trade-off, say so — lay out both sides and let me decide.

Keep going until I say I'm satisfied. Don't softball it — I want to see the cracks before they become production bugs.
